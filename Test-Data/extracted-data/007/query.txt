A 25-year-old male was hospitalized for vomiting blood. On examination, he was found to have an enlarged liver and spleen, and endoscopy confirmed the presence of esophageal varices. Blood tests revealed anemia and a significantly elevated eosinophil count. Serological tests for hepatitis B and C were negative, and the patient denied excessive alcohol use. A liver biopsy showed extensive granulomatous changes and fibrosis. Further history revealed he spent his childhood in Kenya where he frequently swam in a lake.
