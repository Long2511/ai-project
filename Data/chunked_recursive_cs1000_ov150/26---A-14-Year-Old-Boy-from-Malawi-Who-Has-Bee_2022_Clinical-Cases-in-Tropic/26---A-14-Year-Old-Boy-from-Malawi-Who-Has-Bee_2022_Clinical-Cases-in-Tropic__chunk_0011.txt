Myotoxic venom, which causes trismus, rhabdomyolysis, myoglobinuria, renal failure, hyperkalaemia, and respiratory failure, is found in sea snakes. Lastly, nephrotoxic venom, causing acute kidney injury and renal necrosis, is associated with boomslang, vine snakes, and saw-scaled vipers.
