Common complications of spinal cord disease and their management include pressure sores, which are prevented by nursing care and guardian training for two-hourly turning. Urinary retention is managed with catheterization. Contractures are managed with physiotherapy and by training guardians for home-based physiotherapy. Pain can be managed with pain relief by NSAIDs or opiates and the involvement of a local palliative care team. Immobilization can be addressed by prescribing walking aids or wheelchairs if available. Depression is managed through spiritual and mental support, occupational therapy or community projects, pharmacotherapy, and the involvement of a local palliative care team.
