In Australia, Barmah Forest virus causes about 2,000 cases per year, with symptoms of fever (50%), rash (40-60%), myalgia (50-80%), and arthralgia or arthritis (70-95%). O'Nyong Nyong virus in East Africa has caused rare, large epidemics, including one with over 2 million cases from 1959-1961. Its symptoms include fever (80-100%), rash (70-90%), myalgia (70%), and arthralgia or arthritis (60-100%). Finally, Mayaro virus, found in South America's Amazonian rainforest and the Caribbean, occurs in sporadic single cases and small outbreaks of 10-100 cases. Its symptoms include fever in 100% of cases, rash in 30-50%, myalgia in 75%, and arthralgia or arthritis in 50-90% of cases.
