Serological testing for alphavirus infections was performed on blood samples taken on August 29 and September 12. For Mayaro virus, the test was positive. The August 29 sample showed an IgM-IFA of 1280, an IgG-IFA of 2560, and a PRNT of 40, while the September 12 sample showed an IgM-IFA of 40, an IgG-IFA of 2560, and a PRNT of 160. For Sindbis virus, the result was negative, with the August 29 sample showing an IgM-IFA of less than 20, an IgG-IFA of 160, and a PRNT of less than 20, and the September 12 sample showing identical results. Testing for Chikungunya virus was negative; both samples had an IgM-IFA of less than 20 and an IgG-IFA of 160, with the PRNT test not performed. Similarly, testing for Ross River virus was negative, with both samples showing an IgM-IFA of less than 20 and an IgG-IFA of 160, and the PRNT test not performed. Finally, the test for Barmah Forest virus was also negative
