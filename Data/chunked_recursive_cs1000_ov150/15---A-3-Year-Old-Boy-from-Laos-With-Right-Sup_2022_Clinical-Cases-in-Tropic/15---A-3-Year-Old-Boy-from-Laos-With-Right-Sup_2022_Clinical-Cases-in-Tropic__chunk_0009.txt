It is believed that ingestion of water contaminated with B. pseudomallei may cause colonization of the oropharynx and ascending infection to the parotid gland, particularly where drinking water is obtained from boreholes. Less than 10% of children have a predisposing condition. The prognosis is usually favourable, although complications include spontaneous rupture into the auditory canal, facial nerve palsy, septicaemia and osteomyelitis.
