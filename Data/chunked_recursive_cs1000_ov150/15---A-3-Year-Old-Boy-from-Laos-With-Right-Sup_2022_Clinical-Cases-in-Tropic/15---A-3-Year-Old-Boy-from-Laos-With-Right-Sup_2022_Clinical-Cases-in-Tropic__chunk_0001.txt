A 3-year-old rural Lao boy presents to the ENT clinic with a 10-day history of gradual, painful swelling of the right cheek with associated fever and poor appetite. Three days previously his mother noticed a purulent discharge from the ear. He has no cough, vomiting or diarrhoea. There is no history of previous ear infection or dental problems and no known history of trauma. The child is developing normally and is up to date with vaccinations. His parents are rice farmers.
