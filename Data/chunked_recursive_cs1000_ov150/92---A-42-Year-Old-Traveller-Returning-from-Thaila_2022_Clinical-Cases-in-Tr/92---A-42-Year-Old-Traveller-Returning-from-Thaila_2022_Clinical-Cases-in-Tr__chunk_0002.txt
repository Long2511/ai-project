Dengue NS1 antigen test is negative, the rapid diagnostic test for malaria (which detects Plasmodium falciparum-specific histidine-rich protein II and the panmalarial aldolase) is negative.

A 42-year old German man presents with a fever for 6 days, retro-orbital pain and arthralgias after extensive travel in southern Thailand. He is febrile, but otherwise physical examination is unremarkable. Full blood count reveals thrombocytopenia, CRP and liver function tests are slightly elevated. Rapid diagnostic tests for dengue and malaria are negative.
