However, DEC should not be given in areas co-endemic for onchocerciasis or loiasis because of potentially severe side effects, and care must be taken to administer ivermectin in areas with Loa loa co-endemicity.

Treatment in mass drug administration (MDA) programmes is recommended annually; whereas individual patients should get treatment every 6 months until microfilariae and CFA tests are negative, or life-long, if transmission goes on.

Doxycycline kills endosymbiotic Wolbachia bacteria, which adult worms require for viability and reproduction. Several weeks of treatment are required, and doxycycline is contraindicated in pregnancy and in children under the age of 8 years, which make it an unsuitable option for mass treatment. Its role may be in individual treatment of LF patients who lack any of the contraindications.
