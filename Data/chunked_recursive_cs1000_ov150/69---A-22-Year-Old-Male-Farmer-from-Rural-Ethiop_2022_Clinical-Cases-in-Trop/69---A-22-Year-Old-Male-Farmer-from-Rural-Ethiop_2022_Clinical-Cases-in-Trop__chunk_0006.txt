Education to avoid consumption of L. sativus and measures to reduce toxin burden are important public health interventions. New cases of this preventable disease continue to occur. Behaviour change communication among high-risk communities can promote positive practices to reduce toxin exposure. Such practices include the use of metallic cooking utensils rather than traditional clay pots to avoid accrued toxicity from iron-induced oxidation, addition of antioxidant seasonings, soaking seeds in lemon water and avoiding unripe seeds.
