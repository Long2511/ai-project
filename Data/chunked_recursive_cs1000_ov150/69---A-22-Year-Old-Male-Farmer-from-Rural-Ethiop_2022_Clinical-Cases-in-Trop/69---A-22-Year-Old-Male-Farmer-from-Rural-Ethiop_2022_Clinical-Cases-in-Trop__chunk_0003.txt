At the age of 20, he moved to the capital city seeking an alternative job. He migrated along with another young male farmer from his village who had suffered a similar fate; he had weakness of both legs and arms, which he had developed during the same period of drought. There was no history of cassava exposure in the region.

An alert young man with a normal mental state. The gait is spastic ('scissor gait'), with foot-dragging and toe-scraping. There is mild bilateral lower limb spastic weakness of pyramidal pattern, with pathological brisk deep tendon reflexes and sustained foot clonus. Extensor plantar response is elicited on the right, while equivocal on the left. Cranial nerves and upper limbs are normal. There is no sensory or bladder dysfunction.

Full blood count, liver and renal function tests, cerebrospinal fluid, nerve conduction studies and electromyography are normal. His HIV-1 serology is non-reactive. HTLV-1 and -2 serology and MRI are not available.
