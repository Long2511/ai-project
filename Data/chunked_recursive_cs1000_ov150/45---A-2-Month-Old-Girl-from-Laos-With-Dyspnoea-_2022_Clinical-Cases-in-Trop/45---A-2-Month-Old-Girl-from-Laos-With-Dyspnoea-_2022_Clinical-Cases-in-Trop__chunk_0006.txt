A thiamine-deficient diet is largely made up of milled, sticky rice that has had most of its thiamine removed as a result of the milling process. In the late nineteenth century, the advent of mechanical rice milling, which removes the main dietary source of thiamine in the rice husk, is thought to be the precipitant for beriberi becoming a major public health problem in Asia, responsible for considerable mortality. However, there has been very little recent epidemiological research, despite evidence that it remains focally important.
