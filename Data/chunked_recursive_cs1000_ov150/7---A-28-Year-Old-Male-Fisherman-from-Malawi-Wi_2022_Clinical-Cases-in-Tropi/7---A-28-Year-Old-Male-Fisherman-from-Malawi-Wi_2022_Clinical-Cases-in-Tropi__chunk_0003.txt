The patient's laboratory results on admission showed a white blood cell count of 3.8 x 10⁹/L against a reference range of 4–10, haemoglobin of 6.0 mg/dL against a range of 13–15, and a mean corpuscular volume of 92 fL within the 80–99 range. The platelet count was 187 x 10⁹/L, which is within the reference range of 150–400. The creatinine level was 1200 μmol/L, far exceeding the reference of less than 120 μmol/L, and the blood urea nitrogen was 89.3 mmol/L, compared to a reference of less than 17.9 mmol/L. The potassium level was high at 7.2 mmol/l, outside the reference range of 3.5–5.2 mmol/l.

A young Malawian fisherman presents with signs and symp toms of left ventricular heart failure, hypertension and anae mia. There is renal angle tenderness, and his kidneys appear enlarged. His creatinine is very high and he is hyperkalaemic.
