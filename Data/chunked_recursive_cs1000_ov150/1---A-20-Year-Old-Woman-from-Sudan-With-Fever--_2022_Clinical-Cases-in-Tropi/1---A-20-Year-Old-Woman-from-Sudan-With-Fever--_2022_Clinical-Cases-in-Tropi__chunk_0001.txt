A 20-year-old housewife presents to a hospital in northern Uganda with a 2-day history of fever, severe asthenia, chest and abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, diarrhoea and slight non-productive cough. The patient is a Sudanese refugee living in a camp in the region. She denies any contact with sick people.

The patient is prostrate and semiconscious on admission. Vital signs: temperature 39.6°C, (103.3°F) blood pressure 90/60 mmHg, pulse 90bpm, and respiratory rate 24 cycles per minute. Physical examination revealed abdominal tenderness, especially in the right upper quadrant, hepatosplenomegaly and bleeding from the gums. The lungs were clear. No rash or lymphadenopathy was noted.
