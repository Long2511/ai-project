Upon inspection of the eyes, it was found that the right eye had fibrotic lids with a loss of lid architecture and tightening of the palpebral aperture, as well as a loss of lashes from the lower lid and misdirected, in-turned upper lid lashes, known as trichiasis. The left eye presented with swollen lids, mucus deposits, misdirected eyelashes, and eversion of the lower lid margin. The conjunctiva of the right eye was severely inflamed with more than five follicles identified on eversion of the lids and scarring of the upper tarsal conjunctivae. The cornea of the right eye showed diffuse corneal opacification or scarring with central thinning, an irregular surface, and a fibrovascular pannus on the upper cornea. The left cornea had focal inferocentral corneal scars, one with an adherent iris indicating a previous perforation, and a thin central cornea.
