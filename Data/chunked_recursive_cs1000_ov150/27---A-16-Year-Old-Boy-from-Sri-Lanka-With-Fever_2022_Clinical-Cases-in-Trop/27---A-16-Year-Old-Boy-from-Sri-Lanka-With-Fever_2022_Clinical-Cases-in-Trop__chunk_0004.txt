A Sri Lankan teenage boy presents with fever, severe body aches and a reduced urine output. He is jaundiced with sub-conjunctival haemorrhage and has a tender hepatomegaly. He has had contact with a water stream before his ill health.
His laboratory results show signs of cholestasis with only mildly elevated transaminases. The creatinine is increased twofold and there is thrombocytopenia. The leucocyte count is normal.
