Blood test results from a first serum sample, obtained 6 days after the onset of symptoms, and a second sample, obtained 7 days later, were analyzed. For Dengue, the Dengue IgM IIFT test was negative in both samples. The Dengue IgG IIFT test was 1:20 in the first sample and rose to 1:5120 in the second, which was interpreted as cross-reactivity. The Dengue NS1-Ag test was negative in the first sample and not performed on the second. For Chikungunya, the IgM ELISA test was negative in the first sample. The IgM IIFT and IgG IIFT tests were negative in the second sample; these tests were not performed on the first sample. The overall interpretation for Chikungunya was negative. For Zika, the IgM IIFT test was negative in the first sample but became positive with a titre of 1:640 in the second. The IgG IIFT test was 1:20 in the first sample and rose to 1:5120 in the second, indicating a positive result. The Zika RT-PCR was positive in the first sample and was not performed on the second
